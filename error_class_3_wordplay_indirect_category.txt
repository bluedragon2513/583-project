ERROR CLASS 3: Wordplay and Indirect Category Mapping
======================================================

QUESTIONS IN CLASS: 12
CATEGORIES AFFECTED: '80s NO.1 HITMAKERS (5), "TIN" MEN (4), NAME THE PARENT COMPANY (5),
  HISTORICAL QUOTES (3)

EXPLANATION:
Several whole categories require a lookup or reasoning step that is fundamentally
beyond vocabulary-based retrieval. The clue text maps to the wrong article class
because the system has no way to follow the intended indirect mapping.

  Sub-type A — Song title → Performing artist ('80s NO.1 HITMAKERS, all 5 wrong):
    The clue gives a year and song title. BM25 matches the song title's words against
    unrelated articles. The artist's Wikipedia page doesn't rank highly because the
    song is one of many mentioned there. A structured song-to-artist database (e.g.
    Billboard, MusicBrainz) is needed — free-text retrieval cannot bridge this gap.

  Sub-type B — Hidden TIN substring ("TIN" MEN, all 4 wrong):
    The correct answer always contains "TIN" embedded in it: TINtoretto, puTIN,
    marTIN, TINker. BM25 has no substring awareness; it retrieves the topically most
    relevant article regardless of whether "TIN" appears in its title. Three of four
    predicted articles have no "TIN" in their title at all.

  Sub-type C — Brand → parent company (NAME THE PARENT COMPANY, all 5 wrong):
    The clue is simply a brand name. BM25 returns the brand's own Wikipedia article
    (perfect overlap) rather than the parent company's article, which mentions the
    brand only incidentally among dozens of subsidiaries. Structural parent/subsidiary
    knowledge is required, not document similarity.

  Sub-type D — Quote context → speaker (HISTORICAL QUOTES, all 3 wrong):
    Each clue describes the setting of a historical quote. BM25 matches the setting
    vocabulary (event, date, audience) and retrieves the event article rather than the
    article about the person who uttered the words.
