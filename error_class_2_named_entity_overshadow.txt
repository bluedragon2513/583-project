ERROR CLASS 2: Named Entity in Clue Overshadows the Answer
===========================================================

QUESTIONS IN CLASS: 16
CATEGORIES AFFECTED: POETS & POETRY, THE QUOTABLE KEATS, NEWSPAPERS, UCLA CELEBRITY
  ALUMNI, GOLDEN GLOBE WINNERS, THE RESIDENTS, OLD YEAR'S RESOLUTIONS, NOTES FROM THE
  CAMPAIGN TRAIL, I'M BURNIN' FOR YOU, AFRICAN-AMERICAN WOMEN, HISTORICAL HODGEPODGE

EXPLANATION:
Jeopardy clues routinely name a prominent person, work, or place *as context* while the
actual answer is something associated with that entity. BM25 scores the named-in-clue
entity highest because it has perfect vocabulary overlap with the query; the answer
article scores lower because it mentions the context entity only incidentally.

The answer is always "one hop" away from what BM25 retrieves:
  clue names X, answer is something X wrote / appeared in / supported / was associated with.

This is structurally unfixable by BM25 tuning alone. A solution requires the system to
(a) detect that the clue's prominent named entity is context rather than the answer, and
(b) use the named entity as a pivot to search for the associated article instead.

EXAMPLES:

Q14  | GOLDEN GLOBE WINNERS 
     | Clue: In 2009: Joker on film
     | Gold: Heath Ledger    
     | Predicted: Joker (playing card)
     | "Joker" dominates retrieval; the Joker (playing card) article outranks Ledger.
     
     The answer is the actor who played the role, not the character.

     |                          | at this Chicago airport
     | Gold: O'Hare           
     | Predicted: Bessie Coleman
     
     Coleman is named in the clue; her article wins. The answer is the airport.

     Philip IV is the prominent named entity; the Templar order is one hop away.
