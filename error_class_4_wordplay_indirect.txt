ERROR CLASS 4: Wordplay / Indirect Mapping
==========================================

EXPLANATION:
Two sub-types of indirect answer mapping that BM25 cannot handle:

Sub-type A — Song title to performing artist ('80s NO.1 HITMAKERS, all 5 wrong):
The clue gives only a year and a song title. The correct answer is the performing
artist, but BM25 matches the song title's words to unrelated Wikipedia articles
whose titles happen to share those words. The artist's article doesn't rank highly
from the song title alone because the song is one of many mentioned in the article.
A fix would be to use a structured knowledge lookup (e.g., MusicBrainz or a song-to-
artist mapping) rather than free-text BM25 retrieval for this category.

Sub-type B — Hidden substring wordplay ("TIN" MEN, 2 of 4 wrong):
The category name signals that every correct answer contains the string "TIN" embedded
in it (TINtoretto, marTIN, Vladimir puTIN, Joe TINker). BM25 has no phonetic or
substring awareness. It retrieves the most topically relevant article, which is often
a different famous entity in the same domain (Italian painters → Leonardo da Vinci;
Beatles producers → "Fifth Beatle"). The two correct answers in this category (Putin,
Tinker) happened to be the most BM25-prominent match for their clues even without the
wordplay signal. The two failures occurred where a different famous entity dominated
the retrieval.
