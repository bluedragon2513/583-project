ERROR CLASS 5: Right Entity Type, Wrong Specific Entity
========================================================

EXPLANATION:
The largest error class (39 questions). The retrieval lands in the correct semantic
neighborhood — it finds a real article about a person, place, or organization that is
genuinely related to the clue — but it is the wrong specific one. Two sub-patterns
appear:

Sub-type A — Clue entity retrieved instead of answer entity:
The clue mentions a person, place, or work by name as context, and BM25 promotes
that named entity's article to the top, even though the question is asking for
something *about* or *associated with* that entity. The answer is one hop away from
what BM25 finds.

Sub-type B — Most-famous entity in domain retrieved:
BM25 retrieves the highest-scoring article in the right domain (most famous city in
a region, most prominent alumni of a university, most decorated actor) rather than the
specific one the clue points to. This is especially visible with UCLA CELEBRITY ALUMNI
(Kareem Abdul-Jabbar appears as the predicted answer for multiple different questions)
and AFRICAN CITIES (Marrakesh and "History of South Africa" appear when Casablanca
and Cape Town are needed).

A partial fix for sub-type A would be to detect when a named entity in the clue
dominates the retrieval and penalize it at reranking time. Sub-type B is harder;
it requires the reranker to discriminate within a domain using fine-grained clue
details, which demands a stronger LLM or more targeted prompting.
