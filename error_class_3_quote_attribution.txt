ERROR CLASS 3: Quote Attribution — Event Retrieved Instead of Speaker
======================================================================

EXPLANATION:
All three HISTORICAL QUOTES questions are wrong. Each clue describes the historical
context in which a famous quote was delivered. BM25 matches the rich contextual terms
(the event, the period, the audience) and retrieves the article about the historical
event or setting, not the person who actually said the words. The speaker's Wikipedia
article is relevant but scores lower because it competes with many other topics in its
content. A targeted fix would be to prompt the LLM explicitly to identify the *person*
who said the quote, or to apply an entity-type filter that prefers biographic articles
over event/period articles when the category is HISTORICAL QUOTES.
