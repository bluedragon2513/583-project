ERROR CLASS 4: Right Domain, Wrong Specific Entity
===================================================

QUESTIONS IN CLASS: ~38 (largest class)
CATEGORIES AFFECTED: most categories

EXPLANATION:
BM25 lands in the correct semantic neighborhood — the retrieved article is genuinely
related to the clue — but selects the wrong specific entity. The clue vocabulary is
strong enough to reach the right topic area but not specific enough to isolate the
one correct answer.

  Sub-type A — Most-famous entity in the domain:
    BM25 returns the highest-scoring article for the topic but that article is a
    different, more prominent entity than the one the clue specifies. Recurring
    examples: Kareem Abdul-Jabbar for any UCLA alumni question, broad history
    articles for any specific historical event, major cities for minor capitals.

  Sub-type B — Thematically adjacent / wrong granularity:
    BM25 finds an article in exactly the right subject area but at the wrong level
    of specificity — the event instead of the person, the genre instead of the work,
    the country instead of the city, the context instead of the answer.

Sub-type A: Most-Famous Entity in Domain:

Q8   | Clue: This woman who won consecutive heptathlons at the Olympics went to UCLA
     |       on a basketball scholarship
     | Gold: Jackie Joyner-Kersee
     | Predicted: University of Virginia

     UCLA + Olympics retrieves university/athletics articles; UVA wins by BM25 weight
     rather than the specific athlete.

Q13  | Clue: The mast from the USS Maine is part of the memorial to the ship & crew
     |       at this national cemetery
     | Gold: Arlington National Cemetery
     | Predicted: USS Arizona Memorial

     "USS + memorial" matches the USS Arizona article, which outscores Arlington
     despite describing a completely different memorial.

Q22  | Clue: Nov. 28, 1929! This man & his chief pilot Bernt Balchen fly to South Pole!
     |       You'll be an admirable admiral, sir!
     | Gold: Richard Byrd
     | Predicted: 1929

     "1929" is a strong BM25 term; the year article wins over the specific explorer.

Q32  | Clue: The name of this largest Moroccan city combines 2 Spanish words
     | Gold: Casablanca
     | Predicted: Marrakesh

     Both are large, well-indexed Moroccan cities. "Largest" should disambiguate
     but Marrakesh scores comparably, and the Spanish-etymology clue carries too
     little BM25 weight to separate them.

Q54  | Clue: This port is the southernmost of South Africa's 3 capitals
     | Gold: Cape Town
     | Predicted: History of Africa

     "South Africa + capitals" pulls the broad history article; "southernmost" is the
     decisive detail but is rare vocabulary with weak BM25 signal.

Q81  | Clue: Attending UCLA in the '60s, he was no "Meathead", he just played one
     |       later on television
     | Gold: Rob Reiner
     | Predicted: Kareem Abdul-Jabbar

     Kareem Abdul-Jabbar is the most UCLA-indexed celebrity in the corpus; he appears
     as the prediction for multiple different UCLA alumni questions regardless of clue.

Q83  | Clue: This less-than-yappy pappy is sixth veep to be nation's top dog after
     |       chief takes deep sleep!
     | Gold: Calvin Coolidge
     | Predicted: Characters of Parks and Recreation

     The cryptic 1920s-newsreel phrasing ("sixth veep, top dog, deep sleep") provides
     no clean BM25 signal; the system returns a completely off-domain article by noise.

     |       resolution re: this island near mainland China

     | Predicted: History of the Republic of China
