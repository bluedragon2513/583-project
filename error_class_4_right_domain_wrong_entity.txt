ERROR CLASS 4: Right Domain, Wrong Specific Entity
===================================================

QUESTIONS IN CLASS: ~38 (largest class)
CATEGORIES AFFECTED: most categories

EXPLANATION:
BM25 lands in the correct semantic neighborhood — the retrieved article is genuinely
related to the clue topic — but picks the wrong specific entity. The clue contains
enough vocabulary signal to find the right domain, but not enough to pin down the
one specific answer among many plausible candidates.

Two recurring sub-patterns:

  Sub-type A — Most-famous entity in domain:
    BM25 returns the highest-scoring article in the correct topical domain. That
    article is a valid, related entity but not the one the clue specifies. Recurring
    examples: Kareem Abdul-Jabbar appears for multiple UCLA alumni questions; History
    of France / Napoleonic Wars / Roman Empire absorb broad historical clues.

  Sub-type B — Thematically adjacent article:
    The retrieved article covers the same topic as the answer but at the wrong
    granularity (event instead of person, country instead of city, genre instead of
    specific work). The discriminating detail in the clue (a year, a nickname, a
    specific role) carries insufficient BM25 weight to separate the correct answer
    from its thematic neighbors.
